The Man for the Job

Hi there, my name is Shane Loewe and as part of a project for my BACS200 web development class, I'm here to tell you why I would be a good candidate to hire for any project you're working on. So like I said, I’m Shane Loewe. I'm from Greeley, Colorado, the place some people are calling Silicon Valley 2. I’m studying Software Engineering at the University of Northern Colorado, which is my county’s Harvard. And I’m a good choice to hire for any project because of these 5 skills. So here are 5 of my skills. I'm well versed in java programming, python programming, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I’ve completed several coding projects for my University studies, and I have good knowledge of back-end programming and algorithm structures. Those are my hard-skills, but nobody’s perfect, and I’ll be the first to admit it. I forgot to switch my laundry and then my clothes smell like mildew. Does that make me a failure? No, but it does make me smell. And I don’t want people thinking I smell, so I learned from that mistake. I don’t want people thinking I’m bad at what I do, either. So, if I don’t know something, or I mess up, know that I will learn and grow from that experience into the person you need. Because I'm going to mess up, but I can set aside my pride to ask for help and learn from my mistakes, unlike some of my competitors. I’m a hard worker, but talk is cheap. There’s not a thing in my life I haven’t worked hard at except not workin’ hard. The first job I ever interviewed for when I was 16, my car wouldn’t start. So, did I give up? No, I figured it out. I started walking to the interview and calling everybody that I could to try to get a ride to the interview, and I ended up making it there and landed the job.Communication. It’s the backbone of society. You think someone invented the wheel without talking about it with someone first? Since I was able to work, I’ve been putting in the hours at customer service jobs, learning how to talk to as many types of people as I can to get stuff done. And it was my passion for communication that led me to studying the Chinese language, as well as some French and German along the way. 我觉得中文会帮我. Perseverance and integrity. I’ve made mistakes. I’ve made professional mistakes. I used to work at a pizza place, and best believe if I messed up an order, the boss wasn’t too happy. But I fessed up anytime it ever happened. I take pride in everything I do, but I don’t have enough pride to prevent me from being honest, with my boss, with my clients, with my customers, and with myself. I could rattle on about programming all I want, but I wouldn’t be here applying if I didn’t know what I was doing. I have a great foundation of skills that are ready to be stretched and expanded upon. Not only that but I have a great network of incredible software engineers and developers within arms reach who would be willing to help me at any step of the way in your project. Thank you

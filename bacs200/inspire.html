My Hero

It's really hard for me to pick a public figure that I could really call my hero, not because there aren't incredible people in the world, but because I don't know any public figure personally enough to claim that they are my hero. But I think one person who has remained consistent throughout all his years in the public sphere, and someone whom I really admire for that reason is Bernie Sanders. I'm not a fan of idolizing politicians or idolizing people at all, but there is something very alluring about Sanders and his ability to remain consistent with his beliefs since he first entered the political landscape in the early 1980s, and before then in his own personal life. It's one thing for a politician to say or do something that makes themself look good when their under the scrutiny of the public eye, but Bernie Sanders was fighting for what he believed in long before he would break into politics.

At the height of the Civil Rights Movement in the 60's, Sanders moved from Brooklyn to Chicago to fight for civil rights. He would go around putting flyers up all over Chicago, he was arrested by police during protests, he attended the March on Washington, and when he retires, there won't be any more Senators in Congress who were activists for black liberation during the Civil Rights Movement. And the interesting thing? Sanders refuses to talk about it. Bernie Sanders literally chained himself to black activists in the middle of Chicago in the 1960s to protest injustice, something most white people in the country at the time, and quite frankly right now wouldn't dare to do, kicked and screamed at the police as they came to arrest him for such acts, and he doesn't even talk about it. Sanders has never once used his admirable history to further his own political agenda. He is consistent in what he believes, even before anybody was even listening, and he continues to be consistent and influential to this day.

For that reason, I think Bernie Sanders can be a hero for all people who believe in something, and I think his place in our government is important. Thank you for reading my piece about Bernie Sanders, the most consistent man in Congress.

Wealth Inequality on the Rise in US

by Shane Loewe

Right now, the political climate in America is tense. Amidst the global pandemic, political polarization is at an all time high and it is difficult for us as a society to agree on anything, let alone our government leaders agreeing with each other on anything. Although, despite the polarization, there is one thing that is sure to scare any American, or 95% of Americans, rather. Income inequality in America has been rising steadily since the 1980’s and doesn’t show any signs of slowing down, this means that as working and middle class household income is decreasing or remaining stagnant, the wealthy keep getting wealthier. What are the implications of this?

Social Implications

As recent as 2020, the top 1% of households in the United States held fifteen times more wealth than the bottom 50% of households combined. Jeff Bezos, the richest man in America, makes $3,715 per second while 57% of Americans are ONE unexpected $500 expense away from falling into debt. Yet wildly, most of us aren’t thinking about this in our day-to-day lives, but perhaps we should: and not just working and middle class individuals, but the upper-working class as well.

TED Talks

Hans Rosling - The best statistics you've ever seen

Hans Rosling, a professor at the Karolinska Institute in Sweden, discusses the visualization of data and how important
it is to really understand the statistics we collect.

  • Statistics can be boring and hard to read.
  • Synthesizing statistics in our head can lead to misleading results.
  • visualizing statistics lets us break down statistics into ways we can understand them better.

Raj Shah - Using Video Game Principles to Improve Math Scores

Raj Shah talks about using video game design principles to get kids engaged in mathematics the way
they engange with video games.

  • A "Game" is just an objective with unnecessary obstacles.
  • Math is a game in this sense.
  • We can make kids curious about math to make them want to go through the unnecessary
    obstacles of math to solve the problem.

Marc Green - How to Talk Like a Native Speaker

Marc Green talks abou the tips and tricks you can use to achieve fluency in a language on the level of a native speaker.

  • Minimize your accent. Master pronounciation.
  • Learn colloquialisms and idioms.
  • Become familiar with the culture.

Language as a Window into Human Nature

This video talks about how the language we use can give us a glimpes into our relationship with others.

  • We veil our intentions to people dispite the fact that both parties understand the intentions.
  • Our relationships follow 3 patterns: Dominance, Commonality, and Reciprocity
  • Awkwardness stems from two parties who have a different idea of their relationship to one another.
  • You can "take-back" vague language. You cannot "take-back" overt language.
